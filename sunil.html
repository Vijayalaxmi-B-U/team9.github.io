Theory: What Is the Traffic Management Problem?

Cities struggle with congestion, delays, accidents, and wasted fuel. Traffic movement can be viewed as a graph problem:

  • Intersections → Nodes
  • Roads → Edges with weight (travel time, congestion, distance)

Our goal is to find the fastest, safest, least congested movement of vehicles across this graph. We use only algorithms from your allowed list that fit real traffic behaviour.

1. Shortest Route Calculation

Problem

Drivers need fastest routes based on live congestion. Wrong routing increases traffic jams.

Algorithms Used

  • Dijkstra — best for live travel-time routing
  • Bellman–Ford — handles dynamic penalties (accidents, delays)
  • Floyd–Warshall — precomputes all-pairs route matrix for emergency vehicles

Why these algorithms help

They update travel times and compute fastest paths quickly across thousands of intersections.

Benefit to City

  • Faster travel for all
  • Lower fuel consumption
  • Less congestion spread

2. Traffic Signal Optimization

Problem

Signals often stay green even when no vehicles present → wasted time and long queues.

Algorithms Used

  • AVL Tree — maintain real-time vehicle counts sorted
  • Heap Sort — prioritise most congested junctions
  • BFS — check how congestion spreads to nearby intersections

Why these algorithms help

AVL tree & heap sort give fast priority updates. BFS shows how queues propagate through neighbouring roads.

Benefit to City

  • Shorter waiting time at signals
  • Reduced queue length
  • More predictable traffic flow

3. Accident & Road Blockage Detection

Problem

When a road closes due to accident, the city must quickly check which areas become unreachable.

Algorithms Used

  • DFS — detect unreachable zones
  • Union–Find — quickly check if the road network is still connected
  • Bellman–Ford — re-route with updated penalties

Why these algorithms help

DFS + Union-Find instantly detect disconnections and isolated areas.

Benefit to City

  • Fast emergency response
  • Better path redirection
  • Lower congestion after accidents

4. Traffic Sensor / Camera Network Construction

Problem

Sensors must be installed city-wide with minimum wiring cost and maximum coverage.

Algorithms Used

  • Prim’s MST — build minimum wiring cost network
  • Kruskal’s MST — alternative MST with sorting

Why these algorithms help

MST guarantees minimum cost sensor network.

Benefit to City

  • Cheaper infrastructure
  • Better monitoring
  • Reduced maintenance cost

5. Sorting & Data Handling

Problem

Traffic data (speed, congestion, density) must be sorted quickly for reports and dashboards.

Algorithms Used

  • Merge Sort — stable, fast sorting for large traffic datasets
  • Quick Sort — fast for live updates

Benefit to City

  • Better analytics
  • Faster dashboards for traffic control rooms

Summary: How Algorithms Improve Real City Life

  • Reduced congestion through optimal routing
  • Smarter, responsive traffic signals
  • Faster accident response
  • Cheaper and efficient sensor infrastructure
  • Improved emergency vehicle movement
